Open on time with a TI contractor who has done it before

Tenant improvements in St. Louis are part construction, part choreography. Leases, permits, landlord standards, and inspection schedules all hit at once. We've done it enough times to keep every plate spinning so your opening date doesn't slip.

Tenant improvement services

  • Office build-outs: open plans, private offices, conference rooms, IT rough-in.
  • Retail build-outs: storefronts, fitting rooms, point-of-sale stations, branded interiors.
  • Restaurant build-outs: kitchens, hoods, grease traps, dining areas, ADA restrooms.
  • Medical & dental: exam rooms, sinks, sterilization rooms, ADA compliance.
  • Salon, fitness & service spaces: plumbing rough-in, mirrored walls, durable finishes.
  • White-box conversions: full demo to a clean shell ready for the next tenant.

What sets our TI work apart

  • One project lead from kickoff to certificate of occupancy.
  • Direct coordination with landlords and architects on scope and standards.
  • Milestone schedule with weekly progress updates.
  • In-house framing, drywall, finish carpentry, welding, and steel.
  • Vetted local subcontractors for electrical, plumbing, HVAC, fire, and low-voltage.

Frequently asked questions

How long does a typical St. Louis tenant improvement take?

Most TI projects take 4–10 weeks from permit approval to certificate of occupancy. Restaurants and medical spaces with heavy MEP work can run 10–16 weeks. We provide a milestone schedule before mobilizing.

Will you work with my landlord and architect?

Yes. We coordinate directly with property managers, landlords, and design teams so the project moves cleanly from drawings to construction with no gaps.

Do you handle restaurant and medical build-outs?

Yes. We have experience with hood vents, grease traps, ADA restrooms, exam rooms, sinks, and the inspection trail those spaces require in St. Louis City and County.

Who pulls the permits?

We do. We pull all St. Louis City, County, and Metro East permits, schedule inspections, and walk through the final sign-off with you.
