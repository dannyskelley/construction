Industrial design-build with in-house steel

Industrial work demands tight tolerances, predictable schedules, and a contractor who can swing a wrench as confidently as they read a drawing. Abstract Construction LLC self-performs structural steel, welding, and finish carpentry, which means fewer hand-offs and fewer surprises on your project.

Industrial services we deliver

  • Warehouse construction: pre-engineered metal buildings, tilt-ups, and conventional steel.
  • Light manufacturing fit-outs: equipment pads, utility rough-ins, process MEP coordination.
  • Mezzanines & equipment platforms: engineered steel decks for storage, packaging, and process equipment.
  • Structural steel & welding: beams, columns, stairs, railings, supports fabricated in-house.
  • Loading docks & dock equipment: levelers, seals, bumpers, concrete approaches.
  • Industrial tenant improvements: office build-outs inside warehouse and manufacturing shells.

Why design-build wins for industrial owners

  • Single contract, single point of accountability with no design-vs-construction finger-pointing.
  • Faster delivery: design and construction overlap instead of running back-to-back.
  • Fewer change orders because the builder is at the table from day one.
  • In-house steel fabrication means real schedule control on the critical path.

Frequently asked questions

What is design-build construction?

Design-build is a single-contract delivery method where one team handles both the design and the construction. It compresses the schedule, reduces change orders, and gives you one accountable partner from concept to completion.

Do you fabricate structural steel in-house?

Yes. We fabricate and install structural steel, mezzanines, stairs, railings, and equipment supports in-house. That keeps fabrication on schedule and on budget.

What kinds of industrial projects do you handle?

Warehouses, distribution centers, light manufacturing, equipment platforms, mezzanines, loading docks, and industrial tenant improvements.

Can you work with my equipment vendors and engineers?

Yes. We coordinate directly with your structural engineer, equipment vendors, and OEMs to make sure tolerances, anchor patterns, and utility rough-ins land where they should the first time.
